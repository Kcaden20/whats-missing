The Mountains

In Ideas to Postpone the End of the World , Ailton Krenak speaks on the important and intimate relationship that people of this land had with their mountains. He states:

"A lot of folks converse with the mountains. In Ecuador, Colombia and some regions of the Andes, you can find places where mountains pairbond and form families."

and also:

"Early in the morning, down in the village, the people look up at the mountain to see if it's going to be a good day or whether it would be best to stay inside."

When I wake up, I'll see if it's sunny or cloudy and wonder what my family's mountain is telling me from a continent away. When in the office with my co-worker on days not going as planned, we'll joke that our mountains told us not to come to work and that our misfortune is a result of ignoring their advice.

My family fled from a region filled with instability and the mountains took them in. They allowed my family to live peacefully, protecting them from the horrors happening nearby. And yet, the pain and embarrassment of that displacement lives on in my ancestors memory and deep in my own body. It is subtle, but still, it pushes us. It says to keep moving, to leave, because if we stay too long, others will decide these things for us.
